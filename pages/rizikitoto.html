Rizikitoto

Help East African orphans become self-sufficient. Visit rizikitoto.org to learn more.

Overview

Riziki, meaning sustenance & toto, meaning child.

Rizikitoto is a nonprofit organization that works to help East African orphans become self-sufficient. We’ve built a network of mobile apps where the revenue of each app is spent on resources that support the kids’ talents, interests, and aspirations.

For two years, Rizikitoto supported over 50 children living in Uganda. By funding initiatives that align with each child's interests, we hope that each child would gain the motivation and self-confidence to pursue their dreams.

Background

My mom and dad were both born in Tanzania. On trips visititing East Africa, my siblings and I would volunteer in Bright Futures orphanage in Kampala, Uganda. We taught kids aged 6 to 17 basic reading, writing, and math skills, while gradually getting to know their outside interests and goals. In working with the kids, I realized that the primary problem was not limited to need, it was self-sustenance. These children were already extremely bright, but they lacked the resources and gudiance to actually follow their dreams. After all, how can a girl with no books for school someday become a teacher?

In March of 2014, my best friend Mariam had a big birthday coming up, so I thought of creating her a Mariam-themed iOS game as her present. This led me to the idea for Rizikitoto: to connect one app to one child and use the revenue from the app to support that child's aspirations.

After integrating 5 iOS games that I built into Rizikitoto's model and creating a partnership with Bright Futures Orphanage, we began to conduct outreach and plan initiatives for the orphanage.
