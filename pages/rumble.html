Rumble

Tackle soil erosion in the agriculture industry.

Overview

Rumble addresses soil erosion in the agriculture industry. Rumble is a spherical device that farmers can insert up to five feet deep into their topsoil depending on the crop's variable root depths. Rumble uses the ports located across its surface to send puffs of pressurized air through the plot and shake the topsoil enough to enable water absorption. Rumble also uses said ports to return minerals to the soil.


Background

Soil erosion is when water and wind continuously deteriorate topsoil, depleting the soilâ€™s fertile material. This results in a lack of water absorption and a buildup of water on the surface of the topsoil. Eventually, this becomes soil runoff which carries pesticides into our water systems and harms habitats.

In the past century, the rate of soil mineral depletion in North America has increased by 85%. Rumble shoots glacial gravel through its ports to restore an array of minerals in the soil. Remineralization is less expensive and labor-intensive than traditional fertilizing methods. One ton of rock dust costs $8.00 whereas one ton of fertilizer costs $400.

Rumble lowers costs by eliminates traditional fertilization and adds the value of remineralization.

Rumble was a collaboration between myself, Will Berman, Monique Manaloto, and Avni Barman. My role focused on design and strategy.
