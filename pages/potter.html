Potter Pics

A Harry Potter themed social network.

Overview

Potter Pics is a photo-sharing social network for the wizarding world. Share pictures, add filters and captions, and find out which Hogwartz houses your friends belong to. Muggles are not welcome.


Ever wonder what Harry, Ron, and Hermione post on social media? Me too! Potter Pics was an experiment that allowed me to explore two of my favorite worlds: Harry Potter and iOS. Simply enter the app with The Unlocking Charm to be sorted into a house and start interacting with the rest of the magical world.

Featured in Natasha Murashev’s iOS newsletter reaching 10,000+ people.

Process

There are countless aspects of the Harry Potter world that could translate to engaging, digital experiences. Because I created this app for fun, there were few feature constraints that I had to work around. As such, I juggled multiple ideas throughout my design process.

I experimented with a sorting house quiz for the user’s onboarding, having an interactive map similar to Marauder’s Map for users to see their friends’ locations, using spells to unlock features, and more.

Though exciting, the lack of constraints had me going in way too many directions. The aforementioned ideas were literal representations of what people know from the books and movies. They were incoherent and failed to serve a real purpose in a simple photo-sharing app.

For this reason, I ultimately decided on the following three subtle, visual Harry Potter integrations:

  1. House Navigation: The user’s navigation and secondary font’s color changes based on his/her Hogwarts house. This allows the user to have a more personalized experience while not getting distracting from the core functionality.

  2. Home Intro: The home page resembles the Harry Potter movie introductions. The music, moving clouds, and iconic text provide a familiar feel for the user as he/she enters the app. The user taps “Alohamora,” also known as The Unlocking Charm, to login or sign up. It makes sense to have obvious, familiar Harry Potter elements for the onboarding so that the user is immediately engaged. I animated the clouds in After Effects to resemble the movie intros and created the text in Photoshop.

Takeaways

  • Nothing takes priority over the user. It's easy to get sidetracked with exciting ideas and unique interactions, especially when working within the context of Harry Potter. However, if such features don't contribute to the goal, to share photos, there is no purpose for them in the app.
  • Create and use custom icons if and only if it is abundantly clear what they represent. This is an area of the app I could have definitely improved upon by establishing early feedback loops and conducting additional user tests.

Tools: Sketch, Swift, After Effects, Photoshop, Illustrator
